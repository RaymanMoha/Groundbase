Strategic Brand Analysis

What should we
call this thing?

A comprehensive naming strategy for the AI-powered field intelligence platform currently called "Groundbase." This is permanent — let's get it right.

The Problem

Why "Groundbase" isn't working.

Critical Issues with the Current Name

  • "Groundbase" sounds like a coworking or workspace product (WeWork, Spacely, HubSpot territory)
  • Zero connection to field operations, AI, intelligence, data collection, or your actual value proposition
  • You don't own groundbase.com — you're at getgroundbase.com, a weaker domain position
  • Impossible to own the SEO narrative — "groundbase" is generic and returns noise in search
  • Doesn't scale with your AI-first positioning. "Groundbase AI" sounds bolted-on, not native
  • Competitors with descriptive names (Fulcrum, FieldPulse, GoCanvas) immediately communicate what they do — you don't
  • In enterprise sales conversations, the name doesn't help your pitch. Prospects shouldn't have to ask "what does Groundbase do?"
Naming Framework

What the perfect name must do.

Since there's no going back once you buy the domain, every candidate was evaluated against these seven non-negotiable criteria:

01

Domain Available (.com)

The .com must be buyable — either unregistered or reasonably purchasable. No "get-" prefix workarounds.

02

Signals the Category

The name should hint at field operations, intelligence, data, or ground-level work — without being too literal.

03

Globally Pronounceable

Must work in Nairobi, Lagos, London, Dubai, and New York. No English-only wordplay or cultural blind spots.

04

Enterprise-Ready

The name should feel premium enough for a $69/user/mo enterprise sale and a boardroom presentation.

05

2-3 Syllables Max

Short names win. Think: Slack, Stripe, Notion, Linear. Every syllable is a tax on memorability.

06

AI-Native Feel

Should feel like an AI company from day one — not a forms company that bolted on AI later.

07

Scales Beyond V1

The name should still make sense when you expand into predictive analytics, autonomous workflows, and IoT.

Final Recommendation

My pick for your forever name.

SitePulse
sitepulse.com

It does the most strategic work with the fewest letters. Every operations leader on earth immediately understands what SitePulse does — it gives you the real-time pulse of your sites. It's two syllables, globally pronounceable, and writes its own marketing. Pair it with your existing "Field Intelligent" tagline and you have a category-defining brand.

However — if you want the name that will make investors, enterprise buyers, and the market see you as a $1B+ platform company, go with Verico. It's the Salesforce play — abstract, premium, infinite scalability.
